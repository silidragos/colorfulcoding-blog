<p>
    Working for myself was in the back of my head for like forever. As people ask me what I do now I mostly answer by
    mumbling a combination of working on my own product (<a href="http://insidar.tech">InsidAR</a>), accepting work for
    projects I find challenging and exciting and also a few other things. The truth is I don't really have a masterplan.
    Or, rather the masterplan is to do what I feel would bring me the most satisfaction and eventually even have a
    living out of this. Yep..I've put myself a ticking clock of Six months and I'll see if this kind of living is viable
    for me.
    <br />
    So here is a summary of how my first month went. I'm not sure you actually care about it, and that's ok. I consider
    it a way to look back and try to see what went well and what not. Consider it a Sprint Review, but as for now I have
    no teammates on this, I'll force you, the reader, to be here, as for me to not feel crazy for saying these things to
    a wall or a lamp.
</p>
<h2> Creating myself a routine I'm trying to adapt to. </h2>
<p>
    It's not much, but I have an innate fear of wasting time. Also, it's fun to make a schedule while actually using all
    the hours in a day. Not to go into details, but it's mostly a <b>Morning Routine - Coffee, Reading and Learning -
        Lunch - Work - Dinner - A bit of work - Whatever - Sleep</b> day after day.
    <br />
    It might suffer changes, there are times when I don't feel like it, but it's something to keep me accountable. I try
    to remember I didn't leave work to do less, but more. However, I'm a soft boss for myself. I allow exceptions. I
    actually enjoyed a few free days inside the week.
</p>
<blockquote>Aah, I earned it, who cares it's Wednesday.</blockquote>
<img style="margin-top:100px; margin-bottom: 100px;" src="/images/5/1.jpeg" />

<h2> 6 hours can be a lot! </h2>
<p>
    Yep. You heard me. To actually be fully focused on that piece of code for 6 hours and sometimes more can be a lot.
    And I learned this looking back. Being actually the only person accountable for the success of my projects improved
    my focus. I had it before. While I worked as an employee had its perks and I had days where I was fully in. But I
    can say I never touched so many subjects I wanted to touch, while also feeling more rested. So here are the main
    points I am pretty proud of doing for now.</p>
<ul>
    <li>14 days of working on <a href="http://insidar.tech">InsidAR</a>. This doesn't sound much, I worked much more
        time last year, after 4, when arriving home. However, these were 14 days of 5-6 hours fully involved work, with
        a fresh, rested mind. Let me put it into perspective. Although the foundations were thoroughly laid before, I
        had the time to try a few new techniques, rewrite all of the code and reduce the error from <b> a few meters to
            maybe a dozen centimetres </b>. We're talking about the ideal, small places where I made my tests and it's
        still far from being finished or even production ready. But for me at least those were some huge steps forward,
        I wasn't capable of doing with whatever energy reserve I got left after work and on weekends. <a
            href="https://twitter.com/silidragos/status/1091617082729553920">Checkout the demo here!</a></li>
    <img style="margin-top:100px; margin-bottom: 100px;" src="/images/5/2.jpeg" />
    <li>Meetup and Community. Lots of planning and ideation, mostly, of things that should happen in the future. Not
        really quantifiable but I think the planning this month will matter soon enough :D </li>
    <li> Giving myself at least a couple hours a day where my only duty is to read tech news, tutorials and so on. This
        usually happens on the Coffee part of the daily routine. On the more quantifiable side, I started learning
        <b>Android</b> enough as to help me in what I do, had a look over <b>AR Geolocation Systems</b>, <b> AR on React
            Native</b> and an in-depth look over <b>ARCore</b>.
    <li>Slowly but steadily finishing the work for my dissertation. Yes, soon enough I can be a master! It's those kinds
        of things that are not necessarily hard but it's the last kind of things you want to fit into your free time.
        Especially writing those 40 pages of documentation. I will repeat myself. It gets much clearer when work is work
        and is treated as a task for doing in certain days.</li>
    <li> Had a whole day where I kind of fixed things around this blog that kept annoying me for months. It's still not
        perfect, but..you know.</li>
</ul>
<h2> Events and going out </h2>

<p>I think I walked the most steps I've ever walked in a month, as trying to not get myself isolated in my cosy home and
    accept most of the opportunities, presentations and whatever reason to go out and take some fresh air. It's because
    I no longer have the excuse of "I already socialized at work so I need some time alone". Now, the time alone is
    while working on stuff, and maybe this is better. </p>

<p> I heard many people gave up as staying inside too long can get depressing. I dodged that for now. Who knows about
    the near future, though?</p>

<h2> Experiment more </h2>
<p>As I am the God of my own schedule now I can experiment more. I started building my own green screen and took me a
    whole day. I started to look for all types of things to do while eating. What would happen if I'd sleep after lunch?
    What about meditation at 4 pm while taking a pause? Do I work better at morning or during the evening? </p>

<h2> Conclusion </h2>
<p>
    I'm not saying working at the office doesn't have its perks. I am also not sure I want to work alone my whole life,
    as I still wish for having a passionate team with me in the future, whatever the future will be. But for now, I'm
    starting to think outside the office. I try to understand what does and does not fit with my way of being. We are
    offered a fixed schedule but no one asks if it reaches our full potential. For many of us it does, but we are all
    different.
    <br />
    It all sounds zen, but I still have things to figure out. On the top of my head, I can enumerate how do I get back
    to having the same money as when I was employed? I started working at the product but I also need a team. For now,
    it worked well, but will I manage to keep myself connected to the "outside world"? And all kind of worries. But, one
    day at a time, right? As long as there is a vision all problems have solutions.
</p>

<blockquote>I am definitely not militating for mass-quitting jobs and burning down corporations. Anarchy! No, all I want
    to say is to take a step back and analyze how would you want your days to go by. And if there is something you want
    to add to your schedule, just look for a way of fitting it in. There are enough projects postponed for months and
    months. I was..just a bit more drastic, let's say.</blockquote>
